Dr Chris Paton CV

Dr Chris Paton is a UK-trained medical doctor specialising in clinical informatics/digital health. He is the Course Director for a new, fully-online Postgraduate Diploma in Digital Health at the University of Otago and is also a Clinical Researcher at the University of Oxford.

Following his medical training in the UK, he joined the University of Otago in New Zealand as a clinical lecturer in Health Informatics before being moving to the University of Auckland as a senior research fellow at the National Institute for Health Innovation. He became a fellow of the Australasian College of Health Informatics in 2010, received his Executive MBA from the University of Auckland in 2011, and was elected as a fellow of the UK Faculty of Clinical Informatics in 2018.

He is a co-founder of the "Life-saving Instruction for Emergencies" (LIFE) project. LIFE is an immersive simulation training platform for healthcare workers around the world. The LIFE project won the "Saving Lives at Birth" grand challenge and the "VR for Impact" award at the World Economic Forum at Davos.

Dr Paton has served as an expert advisor on digital health for government funding agencies, charities and philanthropic organisations including the UK's Medical Research Council, the Research Council of Norway, and Parkinson's UK. He is on the editorial board of two health informatics journals: "BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making" and "Sage Digital Health" and serves as an expert peer reviewer for scientific journals including Nature, PLOS One, JPHC, and JMIR. He has also served on the leadership of two International Medical Informatics Association (IMIA) working groups.
